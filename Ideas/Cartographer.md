_Maps the unknown, revealing hidden paths and forgotten ruins. A master of terrain and lore._

A cartographer is responsible for mapping the journey of exploration. Reassembling natural sites or ruins, registering it in maps. The cartograher may auxiliate the party in the harsh task of exploring the unknown.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Level|Title|
|0|Scribbler|
|1|Landmarker|
|2|Chronicler|
|3|Archivist|

A cartographer needs cartographer's tools or roll it's tests with DIS.

## Scribbler

_Maps are just scribbles to you._

- The cartographer rolls the tests for Exploration in every HEX. Successfull in a hex results the cartographer being able of map that hex. See Navigating and Mapping
    
- A party with a scribbler keeps the last two tiles tiles visible while exploring.
    
- At the end of the day, the cartographer may check WIS CD 18 to know if the party is lost.
    

## Landmarker

_You record paths, but mysteries remain._

- Once per day, automatically reveals one terrain feature. Ask DM to automatically reveal something out of ordinary from the current HEX.
    

## Chronicler

_Your maps reveal hidden truths._

- When the party discover a new landmark, ruin or extraordinary site on a HEX (Point of interest), the Landmarker automatically knows one rumor or clue about it.
    
- Once per day, automatically reveals two (instead of one) terrain features. Ask DM to automatically reveal something out of ordinary from the current tile.
    

## Archivist

_No secret place escapes your charts_

- Maps you create or good maps owned by the party decrease the DC of tests of navigation in one level. (Hard becomes average, average becomes easy, and so on).
    
- When the party discover a new landmark, ruin or extraordinary site in a HEX, the archivist automatically knows two rumor and/or clues about it.
